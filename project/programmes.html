Diploma Programmes

Diploma programmes provide foundational knowledge in computing and practical hands-on skills, preparing students for entry-level positions or further studies.

Suitable for

  • Secondary school graduates
  • Students seeking a quicker entry into the workforce
  • Students planning to continue into degree programmes
Course Outline : Core subjects may include Introduction to Programming, Database Fundamentals, Web Development, Networking Basics, and IT Project Management.
Programme Duration : Typically 2–2.5 years full-time; part-time options may also be available.
Entry Requirements : Minimum academic qualifications, basic mathematics proficiency, and sometimes English proficiency.
Career Pathways : IT support, junior software developer, web developer, network administrator, database assistant.
Progression Opportunities : Diploma graduates may continue to Degree programmes within the School of Computing or transfer to related institutions.

Degree Programmes

Degree programmes provide in-depth, advanced computing education, preparing students for professional careers or research in the IT field.

Suitable for

  • Students aiming for technical or professional careers in computing and information technology.
Course Outline : Include subjects such as Data Structures and Algorithms, Software Engineering, Cybersecurity, Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, and Capstone Projects.
Programme Duration : Typically 3–4 years full-time; part-time options may exist.
Entry Requirements : Completion of high school or diploma, minimum grades in mathematics/science subjects, and English proficiency. Some programmes may require interviews or aptitude tests.
Career Pathways : Software engineer, data analyst, network engineer, cybersecurity analyst, AI/machine learning specialist, IT project manager.
Internship Opportunities : Structured internships with partner IT companies to gain industry experience.

Short Courses

Short courses are flexible, skill-focused programmes designed to enhance specific competencies for students or working professionals.

Designed for

  • Working professionals seeking to upgrade skills.
  • Students wanting additional skills outside their main studies.
  • Individuals interested in specific IT competencies.
List of Courses : Include Web Development Bootcamp, Python Programming, Data Analytics, Cybersecurity Fundamentals, Cloud Technologies.
Duration : From a few days to several months depending on course intensity.
Entry Requirements : Usually open to all; some advanced courses may require prior knowledge.
Certification : Certificate of Completion or Professional Certification issued upon successful completion.
Learning Outcomes : Students gain targeted practical skills, project-based experience, and industry-relevant competencies.
